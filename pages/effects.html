Effects are often added to the final sound in subtractive synthesis to add tone, texture, and character. Commonly used effects are reverb, chorus, distortion, and delay. Each have a very specific application and use case and, much like the usage of LFO's and envelopes, can transform a synthetic sound into something more organic and lifelike.

These can be used in a variety of different ways to either make a sound smooth and dreamy, or harsh and aggressive. The sky is the limit!
