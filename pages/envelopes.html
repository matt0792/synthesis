Envelopes are typically used to shape a sound's amplitude over time. These consist of an attack, decay, sustain, and release. This gives the sound a more dynamic and musical feel, mimicking something that could exist physically.

Envelopes, while typically linked to volume, can also be used to modulate filters and effects to add movement and character.
