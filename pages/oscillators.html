An oscillator is a repeating waveform, it can be thought of as a graph of the speakers' movement over time. When a point on the graph is all the way at the top, the speaker is fully extended, and vice versa for a point on the bottom.

Different shapes of oscillators create different distributions of harmonics, which will ultimately play a large part in the overall character of your sound.

One can stack many oscillators on top of each other, creating a rich, complex waveform to be shaped and refined down the line.
