A filter is a tool that can be used to remove or boost certain frequencies in an audio signal, while allowing others to pass through. In a subtractive synth, we can use these filters to shape our complex waveform into something more refined and musical.

Much like oscillators, there are many types of filters that each affect the sound in their own unique way. Commonly used types are high-pass (removes low frequencies), low pass (removes high frequencies), and band-pass (removes frequencies outside a specified range).

Filters can be controlled by external modulators like envelopes and lfo's, to create movement and add character to the sound.
