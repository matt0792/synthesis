An LFO (Low Frequency Oscillator) is a modulation source that generates slow, repeating waveforms, typically far below the range of human hearing. Instead of creating sound, LFO's can be used to modulate parameters such as pitch, volume, filter cutoff, or even another LFO.

This can create a dynamic movement in the sound over time, or generate time-synced effects and rhythms. Either way, they are a powerful tool for adding depth and complexity to an otherwise static sound.
